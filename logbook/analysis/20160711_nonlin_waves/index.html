July 11, 2016 - Are standing waves nonlinear in gain?

Jonathan Kyl

I try to see how standing waves caused by reflections between components could be nonlinear in gain.

Assuming 100% transmission, the sky signal propogates through to the ADC as: \begin{equation} T_{ideal}=G_{IF}\cdot(G_{amp2}\cdot(G_{BP}\cdot(G_{LNA}\cdot(T_{sky} + T_{iso} + T_{LNA}) + T_{BP}) + T_{amp2}) + T_{IF})\quad. \end{equation} We can absorb the gain and noise temperature terms such that \begin{equation} T_{ideal}=G\cdot(T_{sky} + T_{iso} + T_{sys})\quad, \end{equation} where \begin{equation} G \equiv G_{IF} \cdot G_{amp2} \cdot G_{BP} \cdot G_{LNA} \end{equation} and \begin{equation} T_{sys} \equiv T_{LNA} + \bigg(\frac{T_{BP}}{G_{LNA}}\bigg) + \bigg(\frac{T_{amp2}}{G_{LNA} \cdot G_{BP}}\bigg) + \bigg(\frac{T_{IF}}{G_{LNA} \cdot G_{BP} \cdot G_{amp2}}\bigg)\quad. \end{equation} Suppose that the output signal of the IF amp travels to the ADC, but is reflected back to the IF's input, then reflected and amplified again, ad infinitum. The superposition of waves travelling in opposite directions along the SMA cable results in a harmonic series of standing waves that are whole-number multiples of the fundamental frequency supported by the cable. The resultant signal is a function of the effective reflection coefficient $R$, the gain of the IF amp $G_{IF}$, and the harmonic series of modes supported by the cable $G_{hs}$: \begin{equation} T_{reflect}=T_{ideal} \cdot \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(R \cdot G_{IF} \cdot G_{hs})^n\quad. \end{equation} To make this cleaner we recognize that the geometric series must converge (observationally the amp has not blown up), allowing us to define \begin{equation} G_{reflect}\equiv \frac{R \cdot G_{IF} \cdot G_{hs}}{1-(R \cdot G_{IF} \cdot G_{hs})}\quad, \end{equation} such that the signal measured at the ADC is: \begin{equation} T_{meas}=T_{ideal}+T_{reflect}=T_{ideal}\cdot (1 + G_{reflect})\quad . \end{equation} Now if we calculate the ratio $\mathcal{R}$ with this extra term, defined in previous posts as \begin{equation} \mathcal{R}\equiv\frac{T_{meas}(\theta_z)-\overline{T_{meas}}}{T_{meas}(\theta_z=\theta_0)-\overline{T_{meas}}}\quad , \end{equation} we find that if we want \begin{equation} \mathcal{R}=\frac{T_{sky}(\theta_z)-\overline{T_{sky}}}{T_{sky}(\theta_z=20^\circ)-\overline{T_{sky}}}\quad, \end{equation} we require that both $G_{reflect}$ and $G$ are linear on the order of $\Delta T_{sky}$.

It is unclear to me whether this condition is met - will continue to try and minimize the structure in $\mathcal{R}$ with different combinations of amps and attenuators.

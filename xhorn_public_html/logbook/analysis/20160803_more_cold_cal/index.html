August 3, 2016 - More cold load calibration

Jonathan Kyl

Intro

I performed a series of cold load calbration trials using LN2-soaked pyramidal absorber in a wooden box, with the same procedure as before. I wanted to test the hypothesis that standing waves due to reflections off the room's walls and/or the absorber box were the source of all the structure that we saw in the ratio and in the baseline-subtracted calibrated spectra.

The trials took place over two days. The first day I attempted to replicate the results from Chris's trials, and then remove the forebaffle. The second day I wanted to see the effect of scooting the horn slightly closer to the load - after removing the baffle on the first day, I noticed that the edge of the bench was anterior to the aperture of the horn. Finally I attempted to shield the horn from room reflections using not the baffle but an enclosure of eccosorb sheets surrounding the back and sides of the horn.

Procedure

The procedure is to thoroughly spray the absorber with LN2, position it as close as possible to the edge of the bench, and immediately begin integrating while frequency switching. I used the same integration scheme as before, namely

     lab_spec.integrate(s, int_time=960, LO_freq=[9.445,9.5,9.555], LO_time=2)

Tsys is calculated as (y-intercept / slope) of a linear fit between two points of "known" (really tuned) temperature on a temperature/power plot. Points in between these two can thus be assigned a temperature based on their measured power. We calculate T(f) for each channel of each measured spectrum, then fit each to a 4th order polynomial and subtract. Finally we calculate the mean of these residuals. We also calculate the "ratio" as (Pcold - Phot) / (Pintermediate - Pcold). If Tsys and G are sufficiently flat in frequency and linear with input power, this ratio should also be featureless.

There are two days represented here, each with 3 succesful trials. On day 1, the order was as follows:

  • baffle on
  • baffle off
  • baffle off + flexible SMA
The reason for the flexible SMA was due to a crack in the original rigid SMA between the LNA and amp box that I noticed after the second trial. I had a lot of trouble on this day due to up/downlink timeouts between my laptop and the roach. I switched over to sundog which gave rise to a host of other problems which have since been resolved.

On day 2, the order was as follows:

  • baffle off
  • baffle off + scooted forward
  • baffle off + scooted forward + eccosorb enclosure
All trials on this day were with the flexible SMA.

Conclusions

We agree that the eccosorb serves to suppress the long-mode (5m) RF standing waves, consistent with coherent room reflections. We still see some short-mode RF standing waves in the eccosorb trial - probably due to reflections from within the cal box. We ought to redo the baffle on/off trial due to the high noise in the very first trial. We will also connect the corrugated horn to the signal generator and transmit a narrow band signal towards the cal box, and use the pyramidal horn to pick up any reflections. This is the time-reversed analogue of radiation building up coherently within the box and being picked up by the corrugated horn. This can be measured in real time.

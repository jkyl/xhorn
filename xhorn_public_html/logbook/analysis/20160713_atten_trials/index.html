Intro

I investigate the effect of attenuators and a shorter cable from the electronics box to the roach on standing waves (which may well be a nonlinear term in gain c.f. these two posts).

Results

Figures 1 - 4 all show $R$ as a function of frequency over a single scan, each one with a different attenuator configuration. This is the data as I saw it while I was fiddling around. The full range of data is up on orion and is documented in the waterfall plots and field notes.

Some of this structure does average down, as seen in plots 5 and 6. Although it's hard to tell by eye, I thought that figure 3 showed the least structure of all the different attenuator trials (figure 4 being the only trial without any attenuators). It's also difficut to determine what structure is truly static and what will change due to clouds, tempereture etc. After settling on the setup from fig. 3 (6db attenuators + IF) I set the rig scanning indefinitely.

Conclusions

  • Attenuators on either side of a shorter cable seem to diminish standing waves, as seen in the raw spectrum and in the long-term ratio plots (waterfalls + figs 5 & 6)
  • It is tricky to determine in situ what effect tinkering with the electronics has on the ratio, due to clouds, reflections, code issues (no longer), etc. This sort of process is invaluable though.
  • There is still structure in the ratio that doesn't average out over 1.5 hours.
  • Is it possible that this could be calibrated out with the room temp load? Gain nonlinearity may prohibit this.
  • It seems as if the angular precision was much better in fig. 6. This could be because I removed a heavy vounterweight.
